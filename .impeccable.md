# Ulagat — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Ulagat is a Campus OS for BINOM school (Astana, Kazakhstan) and similar elite private schools. Five distinct audiences share one app:
- **Students (grades 5–11)** — the primary daily users. Phones are confiscated during the school day, so they consume the app in two modes: a fast 5–10 min mobile "morning/bus" glance (schedule, substitutions, announcements) and a deeper 15–40 min evening/desktop session at home (clubs, leaderboard, career, chats).
- **Parents** — check their child's schedule, attendance, announcements; registered via an invite code from their child.
- **Teachers** — post tutoring/services, upload olympiad materials.
- **Parliament (student activists)** — run clubs, events, verify achievements.
- **Moderators (vice-principals) & admins** — moderate content, manage schedules and announcements.

The product is bilingual in spirit (UI in English, notifications in Russian) and serves a Kazakhstani audience preparing for ЕНТ (national university exam).

### Brand Personality
Three words: **clear, energetic, trustworthy.** It is a school operating system, not a social toy — it must feel calm and dependable for parents and staff, yet lively and motivating for teenagers (leaderboards, achievements, clubs). Information density is high; the job of the design is to make a feature-rich platform feel effortless and obvious. Emotional goal: a student opening it on the bus instantly knows "what's happening today," and a parent feels their child's school is organized and modern.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light theme (used by anxious parents on phones, teenagers at home in the evening, staff in offices — never a "cool dark dashboard"). The established and intentional visual language, to be kept cohesive:
- **Primary**: violet/indigo (`hsl(262 83% 58%)`), warm amber secondary for reputation/rewards.
- **Surfaces**: near-white tinted background (`hsl(210 40% 98%)`), white cards, soft borders, `rounded-xl`+ radii.
- **Type**: Outfit (geometric, friendly, already loaded) — keep for cohesion across the existing app.
- **Accent icons**: each feature has a signature lucide icon + color (Schedule=sky, Announcements=indigo, Events=amber, Clubs=violet, Lost&Found=teal, Career=rose, Leaderboard=yellow, Prep=emerald, Chats=pink). This color-coding is a brand asset — reuse it consistently so users learn the system by color.
- NOT: dark glowing dashboards, gradient text, neon, cluttered chrome.

### Design Principles
1. **Reduce surface area, not capability.** A feature-rich app should expose few things at once — group secondary destinations behind a clear "More" menu and a personal cabinet, never amputate features on mobile.
2. **One tap to "me."** The avatar is the user's anchor — tapping it goes straight to their personal cabinet (home base for their data, settings, and sign-out), not a dropdown.
3. **Color-coded wayfinding.** Reuse each feature's signature icon+color everywhere it appears so users navigate by recognition.
4. **Mobile is the morning glance.** On phones, prioritize a fixed bottom tab bar of the 4–5 things a student needs in transit; everything else lives one tap deeper.
5. **Teach the interface.** A guide/overview page and instructive empty states explain what every section does — onboarding is part of the UI, not an afterthought.
